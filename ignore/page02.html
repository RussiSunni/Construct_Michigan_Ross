COURSE OVERVIEW

ACC 534 equips students to use accounting-based information to make better management decisions and increase the effectiveness and efficiency of firm activities.

This course covers two main themes:

Cost Systems
The first part of the course will illustrate how cost systems are used in decision-making, highlight key problems commonly associated with cost systems, and show how firms may go about designing better systems. 

Management Control
The second part of the course will focus on how best to design and implement formal control practices (e.g., budgeting, variance analysis, and transfer pricing), to plan and coordinate actions and decisions, and to motivate employees to effectively and efficiently use firm resources.

 

COURSE OBJECTIVES

Upon completion of this course, students should be able to:

  • Apply core concepts of managerial accounting to accounting-based data to guide management decisions and improve firm performance,
  • Critically evaluate managerial accounting practices as solutions to economic problems, and
  • Design and adapt management accounting practices to address new situations.
